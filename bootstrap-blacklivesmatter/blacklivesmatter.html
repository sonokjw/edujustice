Black Lives Matter

by Michelle Laqab

What Is Black Lives Matter? Black Lives Matter, or BLM, is an activist movement geared towards advocating against the violence against African-Americans.

The Black Lives Matter movement started in 2012, after the murder of Trayvon Martin and George Zimmerman was aquitted for his crime. The movement calls attention to the fact that there is still a lot of Anti-Black beliefs and unfair treatment to all African American's in our society today.

Black Lives Matter has many events, including protests, panels, Twitter chats, social media publicizing, speeches, and events. On their website, users can search for events to participate in, or even submit an event they'd like fellow activists to join in. One of the goals of BLM is to advocate for the rights African American's deserve. It is to pave a way to understand that African American's are the most unfairly treated and targeted race in American society today, and that law enforcement and the way they treat African Americans is wrong and unrightous. It is to bring not only the African American community together, but for everyone to see the unjust actions and to help make a difference.
