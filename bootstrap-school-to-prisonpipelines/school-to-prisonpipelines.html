School-to-Prison Pipelines

by Michelle Laqab

School to Prison Pipeline : Where children are funneled out of public schools and into the juvenile and criminal justice systems. Most of these children have learning disabilities or histories of poverty, abuse, or neglect. They need additional educational and counseling services but they are pushed out of schools due to the zero tolerance policy schools implement.

Examples of Children Getting Kicked Out of School:

  • Bringing nail clippers to school
  • Displaying violent behavior
  • Stealing

Most of the time it is not school faculty who deals with these students - it is the police. They often have little to no experience dealing with juveniles and they are a main reason why schools have a school to prison pipeline. The quickest route from school to a prison is through in school arrest by the police officers employed by schools. The officers aren't not shed any light into the lives of these students, or figure out where the behaviors of these children stem from. They make assumptions about the children because they it is the easiest and fastest way to deal with a child. What needs to be done is that the situation needs to be taken in with more thought, so that not only can the teacher and officer understand why the student acts a certain way, but for the students to learn from their actions and move forward.
